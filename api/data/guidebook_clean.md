## Forms
(page: 6)

- When you want to submit a form you need to fill it digitally and submit it to BAU help center online with the relevant documents.
- Usually you don't need to submit forms as physical copies, and if that is required they will send you an email within 3 days about your request.
- You can still submit it physically to your department secretary but before doing that you always must send it to BAU help center.
- In order to get the digital copies of the forms scan the QR code provided on top of the page.

## Things You Should Know — UMIS
(page: 7)

- A student web application BAU uses for most student information.
- It contains basically all the student information, such as courses, schedules, GPAs, slots.
- You can also request documents from UMIS:
  1. Click Document Request
  2. Fill in the necessary information (select "wet signature" if you need a document with a stamp and a signature)
  3. Click save and after the request in a couple of hours it gets ready.
- To check your requests from UMIS:
  1. Click My Requests
  2. Swipe left to see Document Request
  3. Check if the document is ready.
  4. Pick it up from Student Affairs.

## Things You Should Know — Syllabus
(page: 8)

- The syllabus is a document shared by every course professor.
- It contains important information about things you need to know throughout the course.
- It must contain the grade distribution of the course, deadlines, topics.
- The first thing you need to do when starting a course is read the syllabus.

## Things You Should Know — The Curve
(page: 9)

- It is a grading system used by most of BAU courses.
- Most of the courses do not have a set passing grade.
- The passing grade depends on the average grade of the class.
- The coordinator of the course will assign the passing grade after the final exams are conducted.
- The overall grades of all students in that course will be calculated and the average of the class will be taken to be able to determine the passing grade.
- Not all courses use the curve, some will have pre-assigned passing grades.

## Things You Should Know — NA
(page: 10)

- Stands for = Not Attended.
- Some courses have a rule of absenteeism limit.
- If the limit of absenteeism is exceeded you will automatically fail that course by receiving NA.
- You will not be able to sit the final exam of that course when you receive NA.
- You will be able to repeat that course in the next available semester.
- Usually when repeating a course you failed you don't have to attend the lessons. However, when you repeat a course due to NA you must not exceed the absenteeism limit.
- A lot of courses don't apply the NA rule that is why it's important to ask the professors and read the syllabus.

## Course Registration
(page: 11)

- First year regular students will have their courses added to the system automatically.
- Starting from second year the system allows the students to choose their own sections and courses (make their own class programs).
- The system will open 3 times in each semester:
  1. Early registration period
  2. Late registration period
  3. Add-Drop week (usually the second week of each semester)
- There are payment deadlines for each registration period.
- Check the academic calendar for the exact deadlines and dates.

## Elective Courses
(page: 12)

There are 3 types of electives:
1. General electives (GE)
2. Non-departmental electives
3. Departmental electives

- Each department requires a different number of each. You usually start taking them in your 2nd year.
- Each elective has a limited quota that will be added gradually until add-drop week.
- To check details: go to your department website → Course Structure → select elective type → press on course code → check details.
- Tip: don't choose a course because someone said it's easy, hard for them might be easy for you and vice versa. Take your time, research, and make a list of prioritized electives you wish to take if available.

## Summer School
(page: 13)

- It is where you can take courses during the summer break at BAU.
- Each course lasts about 1.5–2 months and requires an extra fee based on its credits.
- You can take up to 10 credits in total.
- Students usually join to raise their GPA, graduate early, or retake failed courses.
- Not all courses open in summer. The university announces a list of available courses before registration.
- You can find it by searching "BAU summer school courses (year)" online or checking the BAU website.
- To learn summer school fees per credit you need to visit the financial affairs office, or to calculate it you need to divide your semester tuition fee by 20: 4100$/20 = 205$ per credit.

## Scholarships
(page: 13)

- Success Scholarship: After four semesters, students who rank 1st or 2nd in their department receive a 100% or 50% scholarship for the following year.
- Siblings Discount: 10% tuition discount for siblings studying at the same academic level.
- Faculty Scholarships: Available for selected faculties and programs.
- Master's GPA Scholarship: Students with a 2.75/4.00+ bachelor's GPA may receive a scholarship (excluding Distance Education programs).

## Credit Load & Course Rules
(page: 13)

- Courses have National Credits (based on weekly class hours) and ECTS credits (based on total student workload). A full semester is typically 30 ECTS.
- Students must follow the maximum credit limit set by the university each semester.
- Students can reduce their course load (except in the first year) with approval but must take at least two courses per semester.
- Students with a GPA of 3.50+ can take one extra course, while those with a GPA of 3.75+ can take up to two extra courses.
- Students close to graduation may be allowed one additional course beyond the credit limit.
- During summer school, students can take up to four courses (maximum 10 national credits), with an extra course allowed if it is needed for graduation.

## Coursera
(page: 14)

- Coursera is used in many BAU courses as part of your grade (usually 15–20 points).
- Even if you don't have a Coursera-assigned course, you can still use it for free.
- Just email BAU GO, and they'll give you access to choose and enroll in any course or topic you like.
- If your course uses Coursera, you'll get an invitation email from your course coordinator.
- Coursera details (dates, grading, etc.) are listed in the syllabus of specific courses.
- Didn't get the email? Contact BAU GO.
